Section 4: MCS Consent & Policies

If you’re 18 or older, you have the right to make decisions about your business with the NDIS. That’s why we need a record of your consent before we share your information with anyone else or let someone else do things for you. You can take away your consent at any time. Let us know by mail, email, in person or over the phone.

Information that we may disclose includes your name, address, phone number, date of birth, NDIS number, NDIS participant status and relevant shift notes.

MCS securely stores your information in line with the Privacy Act 1988 and NDIS Information Rules. We may share information with family, other providers, or allied health services where it improves your support. You may request access to or deletion of your stored information.

As part of our commitment to continuous improvement, MCS conducts regular audits and surveys. You will be informed ahead of time. You are automatically enrolled unless you opt out below.



I have read and understood the MCS feedback and complaints process:

MCS Feedback and Complaints Process

Participants can provide feedback or make complaints through various channels (e.g., directly to staff, via email, through surveys). MCS may be contacted at: Email: hello@mcsarmidale.com.au Phone: 0491 716 968 Address: 31 Chestnut Avenue, Armidale, NSW 2350 Acknowledging and Documenting: Complaints will be acknowledged within two working days. All complaints will be documented, and the participant will be informed that their complaint has been received and is being investigated. Investigation: Complaints will be thoroughly investigated to understand the root cause and potential resolutions. Resolution: MCS aims to resolve complaints within 15 business days. Escalation: NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission Phone: 1800 035 544 Website: www.ndiscommission.gov.au
